Driving Community Impact

For me I focused with my own university tech community. I decided to focus on allowing and broadening the scope for those who have access to applied based learning. I led a 12-week iOS course and spearheaded the hackathon movement at ASU. The interest and support for these movements were so amazing I had to continue.

With Desert Hacks, after raising $50,000 for the first iteration, we went and wanted to grow the span and outreach of the hackathon. While we made an impact on 200+ students last year in a new and awesome co-working tech space, Galvanize, we want to bring students from across the nation to the largest public university in the world! My goal is to create a more inclusive and diverse tech scene before I graduate in May 2018 from ASU.

With CodePath, I not only petitioned to have it on the ASU campus by obtaining 100 signatures but also led a class of 20+ students through a rigourous iOS bootcamp that is used to train Senior Engineers at Silicoln Valley as well as Facebook University Students. I joined on board their non-profit, CodePath.org to expand their university initative by providing iOS and Cybersecurity courses at no cost to a wide range of universities.

I had the amazing opportunity with CodePath to drive impact at Facebook by mentoring students at Facebook University with iOS as well as drove cross-functional partnerships between the Facebook Security Team, Universities, professors, and students.
